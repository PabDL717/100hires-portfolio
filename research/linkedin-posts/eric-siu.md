**Date:** ~June 2026

I spent an hour picking Matt Van Horn's brain about loops, agents, and AI workflows. Here are the 5 biggest takeaways:

1. A loop is just a cron job plus an LLM. What's new is the software can make decisions now. Instead of sending the same report every day, it picks which report matters.

2. Taste matters more than coding. The biggest bottleneck is just deciding what deserves to be built in the first place. The builders still win. But now the builders can be anyone.

3. Skillify everything. If you do something more than once, turn it into a skill. The goal is capturing judgment so it can be reused, refined, and eventually delegated to an agent.

4. Agents move faster through a CLI than a webpage. Every click is another step, every page load another chance to fail.

5. Apply new ideas to your own business. The people who are getting the most out of AI right now are getting good at spotting their own bottlenecks and pointing these increasingly capable systems at them.
